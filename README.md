# 🛡️ Cyber Agent — Learn Cybersecurity Through Play

> *"You learn by playing. You stay safe by knowing."*

**Cyber Agent** is an interactive educational game designed to teach students aged 11–14 how to recognize and respond to real-world online threats — through gameplay, not lectures.

---

## 🎮 About the Game

Cyber Agent places players in a 2D desktop environment equipped with a computer, phone, and notebook. Each mission presents a realistic scenario where the player must make decisions to identify or prevent a cyber threat.

The game is structured around **3 levels**, each containing **3 missions** of increasing difficulty:

| Level | Theme |
|-------|-------|
| 1 | Social Engineering |
| 2 | Hacking Attacks |
| 3 | Online Scams |

Each level ends with a score summary so players can track their performance and reflect on their choices.

---

## 🎯 Goals

- Help young students identify phishing messages, fake accounts, and social engineering tactics
- Build practical decision-making skills for real digital situations
- Make cybersecurity education engaging and effective
- Serve as a complement or alternative to traditional classroom instruction

---

## 🕹️ How It Works

Players interact with a simulated 2D desktop. Tasks include:

- Spotting a fake or suspicious message
- Deciding whether to click a link or ignore it
- Preventing sensitive information from leaking
- Responding correctly to urgent or manipulative requests

Each correct decision advances the story. Each mistake is a learning moment.

---

## 🛠️ Built With

- **[GDevelop](https://gdevelop.io/)** — a free, open-source, no-code game engine with a visual event-based programming interface. Chosen for its accessibility and focus on gameplay over complex setup.
